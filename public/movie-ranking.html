The Best Movies According to Aditya

My top 3 movies of all-time

Kaakan

  • This is my favourite marathi movie.
  • It shows a pure and innocent love story between two young people from a rural background.
  • The story focuses on village life, traditions, and emotions.
  • It highlights how love is affected by family expectations and social boundaries.
  • The movie shows the importance of simplicity, honesty, and true feelings.
  • Music in Kaakan is soft, emotional, and closely connected to the story.
  • The film strongly represents Marathi culture and rural values.
  • It gives a message that true love is selfless and respectful.
  • Kaakan is appreciated for its realistic storytelling and natural acting.

Ti Sadhhya Kay Karte

  • It is a nostalgic love story based on school and college life.
  • The film shows first love, friendship, and memories of youth.
  • The story moves between past and present, showing how time changes people.
  • It highlights how love is affected by family expectations and social boundaries.
  • The movie beautifully represents 90s era and Marathi middle-class culture.
  • Songs and background music play an important role in storytelling
  • The film gives a message that some relationships remain special forever.
  • Ti Sadhya Kay Karte was loved by audiences for its emotional depth and realism.

Natsamrat

  • It is based on a well-known Marathi play written by V. V. Shirwadkar (Kusumagraj).
  • The story revolves around a great theatre artist and his life after retirement.
  • The film shows themes of self-respect, family relationships, and sacrifice.
  • It highlights how an artist faces loneliness and neglect in old age.
  • Nana Patekar’s performance as Ganpatrao Belwalkar is powerful and emotional.
  • The movie strongly reflects Marathi theatre culture and values.
  • Famous dialogues like “Koni ghar deta ka ghar?” leave a deep impact.
  • Natsamrat gives a message about dignity, gratitude, and human emotions.
